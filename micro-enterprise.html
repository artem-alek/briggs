Micro Enterprise

Small Businesses

Small business is the backbone of our economy. People of all walks of life like the idea of using their unique intrests, talents, and desires to make money by starting and building their own businesses.
Recognizing these entrepreneurial interests among several of its clients, Briggs & Associates developed the Micro Enterprise program specifically designed to assis people with disabilities in realizing their dreams of owning their own small businesses. The program is divided into two phases, start up and growth.

Two Phases

  • Startup
  • Growth

During the startup phase, Briggs & Associates assists in putting together a development plan for their business. This includes helping research potential interest in their product or service, determine materials and/or equipment needed for the business, find a location fort he business if appropriate, and, if needed, locate funding resources for equipment and materials.

Once the business is started, we provide ongoing support in building the business. Example of these services include advice and assistance in marketing including finding new business opportunities for their products/serices, advice on methods to increase their business, and support in resolving business problems when they occur. As with all our programs, the Micro Enterprise program also provides individuals with ongoing personal support and assistance for any unique problems/issues that might arise.

Our clients have proven that the program works by successfuly starting and are building businesses in fine art, photography, laundry services, baked goods, massage therapy, writing/performing arts, lawn maintenance, and personal care services.
