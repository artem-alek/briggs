Project Search

Concept of Project Search

Started at Cincinnati Children's Medical Center in late 1990's but J.Erin Reihel, the hospital's Emergency Department Direct of Nursing
Riehel was looking for ways to relieve the department's overburdened nursing staff of routine tasks and tested the idea of having a person with developmental disabilities do the work. The results exceeded everyone's expectations. In 2003 Briggs & Associates brought the concept of Project Search to Georgia.

The Process

- A collaborative effort with the State of Division of Developmental Disabilities.
- High School transition from school systems in the area.
- Adult employment by providing hands-on training and guidance.
- After the completion of their training, there are opportunities for full-time employment.

Project Search

Results

The program has been a great success. It has resulted in many young people and adults obtaining good, well paying jobs with benefits who might otherwise have had difficulty finding employment in such organizations.
The individuals provide critical support services for the opertaions. For example, in hospitals they provide emergency and operating room supply stocking, equipment and materials processing and stocking, pharmacy packing and documentation services, business/medical records, office support, and patient/guest escort.

- Tennett Hospital System: North Fulton Medical Center. Atlanta Medical Center
- Children's Healthcare of Atlanta: Scootish Rite and Egleston Meical Center
