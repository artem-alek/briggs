Myths & Truths

  • Hiring employees with disabilities increases workers compensation insurance rates?

    Truth is, insurance rates are based solely on the relative hazards of the operation and the organization’s accident experience, not on whether workers have disabilities.

  • Workers with disabilities have more accidents or injuries than other workers?

    Truth is, national studies have shown that workers who have disabilities have no more accidents or injuries than other workers.

  • Persons with disabilities are unable to meet performance standards, thus making them a bad employee risk?

    In 1990, DuPont conducted a survey of 811 employees with disabilities and found 90% rated average or better in job performance compared to 95% for employees without disabilities. A similar 1981 DuPont study which involved 2,745 employees with disabilities found that 92% of employees with disabilities rated average or better in job performance compared to 90% of employees without disabilities.
