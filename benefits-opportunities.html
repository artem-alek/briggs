Benefits for Individuals

People with disabilities are an integral part of every community with the desire to be independent, contributing, and participating members of their communities. However, because of their disabilities or other circumstances, they can be frustrated in their search for opportunities.

Employers recognize their own frustration and expense of maintaining a quality workforce: continual interviewing, hiring, training, and high turnover.
Bringing the two together result in benefits for all.

People with disabilities who become employed enjoy the normal rewards of employment including:

  • Earning money
  • Gaining independence
  • Being a contributing member of the community
  • Learning new skills
  • Improving self-image
  • Developing friendships through interaction with co-workers
  • Expanding their self-image

Benefits for Businesses

People with disabilities are an integral part of every community with the desire to be independent, contributing, and participating members of their communities. However, because of their disabilities or other circumstances, they can be frustrated in their search for opportunities.

Employers recognize their own frustration and expense of maintaining a quality workforce: continual interviewing, hiring, training, and high turnover.
Bringing the two together result in benefits for all.

It's proven that by employing people with disabilities companies have:

  • Discovered quality, dedicated employees
  • Received assistance in training and development
  • Acheived reduced turnover
  • Earned allotted tax credits
  • Enjoyed improved public relations and image
  • Enhanced their company team attitude
  • Improved staff/co-worker communication, and interaction
  • Realized the broad-based value of being involved in a meaningful community leadership role

There is no cost to employers for Briggs & Associates services. Funding comes from federal, state, and local programs. For the employer it only takes a desire to make a difference, to provide the opportunity for people with disabilities to prove, and improve, themselves.
